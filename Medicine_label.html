Medicine label Information Redesign for Elderly

Information design

Through conducting in-depth user test with elderly, we redesign the information on the medicine label of Hospital Authority by choosing the right font, layout, visual hierarchy, order of information and pictogram.

Medicine bottle  
4.5cm x 9.5cm & 3.5cm x 7.5cm

Medicine bag
 7cm x 11cm & 6cm x 11cm

Eye drop and ear drop
83mmx 23mm

Ointment
Diameter: 3.2cm

Pictograms

01

Card sorting of pictogram

By reviewing different colour and design of the pictogram, we finalise 4 sets of different pictograms, including the period of the day, before or after meal, dosage and ways of using the medicine that can let the users to interpret the pictograms directly.

02

Font size and typeface test

We have few rounds of test for typeface, font sizes and font families  for users to choose from. We hope to find out the best range of font sizes and typefaces for users throughout the tests and rating sheet.

03

Color test

We have 6 sets of colours, with different tints. We try to find out which colour is most comfortable and can remind the users.

04

Card sorting of information

We ask the users to rearrange the the importance of information into sequence and group into categories.

05

Layout test 

By giving scenarios to the users, they have to find the answer of our question to check the hierarchy and readability of the layout.

Experimental medicine box

Product design

3D rendered by group mate

Considering the living style of the interviewed elderly and Coronavirus, they want a medicine box to be portable, hygienic and has function of reminder.
